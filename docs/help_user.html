Inventory Planning App — User Guide

This is an online app that does two jobs: it partially automates your Daily Stock Report, and it automatically plans inventory restocking across regions. You drag-drop three Amazon files, fill in a little, and it hands back finished Excel files. This guide explains every button and box on the screen, in the order you meet them.

1. How it works at a glance

  1. Drag-drop 3 files downloaded from Amazon, in their raw CSV format, into the matching boxes: the Sales report (have at least 30 days of sales in it), the Manage FBA Inventory CSV, and the Inventory Ledger (FC-wise stock).
  2. The app validates them and offers a file to download — that is your partially-filled Daily Stock Report (the "daily stock workbook").
  3. In that downloaded workbook, you only fill the In-transit and At the FC (received) tabs. The file completes itself — the Overall-stock report tab builds automatically. This finished file is your daily stock report.
  4. To get the restock plan: drag-drop the completed workbook into the app's Daily stock workbook box, set the days of inventory cover you want (bottom-left), leave other settings undisturbed, and click 🧮 Calculate plan.
  5. The app validates everything and gives you the completed inventory planning file to download.

3. Role 1 — Producing the Daily Stock Report

Step 1. Drop the three Amazon CSVs into their boxes. The app validates immediately and shows three counters — Errors / Warnings / Checks noted. Errors are shown in red with exactly what to fix; with zero errors you get ✅ Amazon files validated.
Step 2. Open the Need a fresh daily stock workbook? section that appears after validation. Keep Prefill Current stock from today's ledger ticked (it copies your live stock in so you don't type it), and click ⬇️ Generate & download fresh workbook.
Step 3. Open the downloaded workbook in Excel and fill only two tabs:
  • In-transit — one row per shipment line. Pick Destination, Mode and SKU from the dropdowns; type Quantity and Cargo-ready date; fill the Actual-reach date when known.
  • At the FC — shipments being checked in at a warehouse: pick Destination and SKU from dropdowns, enter Shipped and Received quantities.
Also glance at the Reference tab's lead-times and correct any that changed. The Overall stock tab fills itself — that completed file is your Daily Stock Report.
Always pick from the dropdowns — never type region names. The Destination cells only accept the exact names the app knows (like Delhi (DL)). Typing DELHI gets rejected by the cell itself. Dates display Indian-style: 07-Jul-2026.

4. Role 2 — Generating the inventory plan

Step 1. With the three Amazon CSVs uploaded (same as Role 1), also drop your completed workbook into Daily stock workbook (filled).
Step 2. Set Days of cover to plan for. Leave every other setting undisturbed.
Step 3. Answer anything the app asks (§5 — most runs ask nothing), then click 🧮 Calculate plan.
Step 4. Review the on-screen results (§6) and click ⬇️ Download plan workbook. That's your completed inventory planning file.

5. Questions the app may ask mid-way

The app never guesses on business judgment — when it needs yours, it pauses and asks on-screen:

The questionWhat it means & how to answer
FC review needed — "MAA4 holds 36 sellable units but is not in the registration file"A warehouse holds your stock but isn't in Amazon's registration list (often a temporary GST/registration issue). Two dropdowns appear: (1) how to treat it — normally Map to a region, then pick which region serves it (for MAA4: Chennai (TN)); (2) whether its stock is currently Fulfillable (can ship to customers — the default) or Not fulfillable (physically there but frozen — it will be shown but not counted). Your answer applies to this run only; once Amazon re-registers the FC and the registration file is refreshed, the question disappears for good.
Sales-window recency — "sales window ends …, stock is as of … — proceed?"Your sales data is much older than your stock data (beyond the configured limit, normally 60 days). Sometimes that's deliberate — e.g. planning on last season's pattern. Tick the confirmation box to proceed; the choice is noted in the output.
ASIN judgment — a product holds stock under two SKUsHappens during a listing suppression, when Amazon creates a temporary SKU for the same product. Choose Combine (suppression will lift soon — count both SKUs' stock) or Only active (long suppression — count only the live SKU; the other's stock is shown as excluded, not lost).

6. The results screen & the downloaded plan

On screen after Calculate: four headline numbers (units planned, demand covered, demand excluded, stockout warnings); any yellow notices (e.g. "this run uses a per-run FC mapping"); Region priorities (which regions need attention first, Pri-1 = most urgent); the Sales velocity vs plan table; the full plan grid; and expanders for line-by-line details, stockout warnings and run information.

The downloaded Excel has five tabs:

TabUse it for
READMEA short orientation note.
Appointment planTHE deliverable — units to ship per product per region, with priorities. Act on this.
Stock snapshotYour current / in-transit / at-FC stock copied in for cross-checking while you work.
Calculation_DetailsEvery number behind every quantity — daily sales, stock parts, rounding, flags. The "why is it 32?" tab. Rows are shaded in groups per product.
Sales velocityPer product per region: Plan qty | sales Velocity/day | Days cover, side by side, region blocks separated by border lines, totals at the end.
Days-cover higher than your setting is normal — full cartons buy extra days, and regions already holding stock show their true (large) coverage. But amber cells on the Sales-velocity tab mean possible OVERSTOCK — coverage far above your setting (more than 1.5×). That's the app pointing at stock piling up against slow sales; review before shipping more there.

7. Common messages & fixes

MessageFix
"contains SKU(s) not in the master template"A new product isn't in the master list yet. An admin must add it (with its box size) to the master file — see the Admin guide. The app never guesses box sizes.
"Destination '…' is not a known region"A workbook destination doesn't match a known region. Re-pick it from the dropdown. (Common city names are auto-corrected in current versions, but the dropdown is the safe way.)
"effective dates are N days apart … refresh the stale file"Your stock files are from different dates. Re-download them for the same date and re-upload.
"Sales window: only N days … below the 30-day minimum"Your sales file covers too short a period for a reliable average. Use a report covering at least 30 days.
"Validation failed — nothing was planned"Fix the red items listed above it and rerun. The app deliberately refuses to plan on faulty data.
Every stop names exactly what's wrong and what to do. Fix that one thing, rerun. The app is built to guide, not puzzle.
