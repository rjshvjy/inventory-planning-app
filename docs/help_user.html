Inventory Planning App — User Guide

This online app does two jobs: it partly fills in your Daily Stock Report, and it works out how much stock to send to each region. You drag-drop a few Amazon files, fill in a little, and it hands back finished Excel files. This guide explains every button and box on the screen, in the order you meet them.

1. How it works at a glance

  1. Drag-drop 3 files downloaded from Amazon, raw (unedited) CSVs, into their matching boxes: the Sales report (at least 30 days of sales in it), the Manage FBA Inventory file, and the Inventory Ledger (FC-wise stock).
  2. The app checks them and offers a file to download — your partly-filled Daily Stock Report (the "daily stock workbook").
  3. In that workbook you fill only the In-transit and At the FC tabs. The rest completes itself — the Overall-stock tab builds automatically. This finished file is your daily stock report.
  4. To get the restock plan: drop the completed workbook into the app's Daily stock workbook box, set the days of cover you want, leave the other settings alone, and click 🧮 Calculate plan.
  5. The app checks everything and gives you the finished inventory planning file to download.

2. Which file goes in which box — read this first

There are four upload boxes, but they are not interchangeable, and the order you use them matters. Getting this right avoids the two most common frustrations.

BoxWhat goes in itWhen
Sales export (CSV)Amazon Fulfilled Shipments report, raw CSVAlways
General stock — Manage FBA Inventory (CSV)Amazon Manage FBA Inventory export, raw CSVAlways
FC-wise stock — Inventory Ledger (CSV)Amazon Inventory Ledger (Detailed), raw CSVAlways
Daily stock workbook (filled)The Excel workbook the app itself generated, after you've filled and saved itOnly for planning (Job 2)

⚠️ Upload the 3 Amazon files first — then the workbook

Add the three Amazon CSVs first, wait for the green ✅ Amazon files validated banner, and only then add the filled workbook. Don't start by dropping the daily stock workbook in — the three Amazon files are what the app builds everything from, so it needs those in place first.

The daily stock workbook goes only in its own box (the fourth one), never in an Amazon box. The three Amazon boxes accept CSV files only, so an Excel workbook won't load there anyway — but keep them straight so you don't get puzzling errors.

Use a freshly-generated workbook, not an old one. The workbook you upload for planning must be the one you generated from this run's Amazon files, then filled and saved — not yesterday's file. An old workbook will be rejected (for example, its "Stock as of" date won't match).

If the screen seems to pause after an upload, it isn't stuck. The app re-reads everything each time you add a file, so a short spinner (up to ~30 seconds) is normal — especially right after you drop a file in. Give it a moment before clicking again.

4. Job 1 — producing the Daily Stock Report

Step 1. Drop the three Amazon CSVs into their boxes. The app checks them immediately and shows three counters — Errors / Warnings / Checks noted. Errors are red with exactly what to fix; with zero errors you get ✅ Amazon files validated.
Step 2. Open the Need a fresh daily stock workbook? section that appears after validation. Keep Prefill Current stock from today's ledger ticked (it copies your live stock in so you don't retype it) and click ⬇️ Generate & download fresh workbook.
Step 3. Open the downloaded workbook in Excel. First, on the Current stock tab, set the Stock as of (date) cell to the date of your Amazon data (usually about 2 days behind today — that's normal). Then fill only two tabs:
  • In-transit — one row per SKU per shipment (repeat the Shipment ID on every row). Pick Destination, Mode and SKU from the dropdowns; type Quantity and Cargo-ready date; fill the Actual reach date when you know it.
  • At the FC — shipments arriving and being checked in: pick Destination and SKU from dropdowns, and enter the Shipped and Received quantities as numbers.
Glance at the Reference tab's lead-times and correct any that have changed. The Overall stock tab fills itself — that completed file is your Daily Stock Report.

The dropdowns inside the workbook — what each one means

DropdownChoices & what they mean
Destination (region)The region a shipment is going to (e.g. Delhi (DL), Bombay (MH), Pune (MH), or BLR4 IXD). Always pick from the list — the cell only accepts the exact names the app knows. BLR4 IXD is a cross-dock hub (a place Amazon uses to redistribute stock onward), not a normal region.
ModeATS = Amazon Transportation Service (Amazon collects and carries it). Self = you arrange the transport. This changes the suggested reach date only: for ATS, suggested reach = cargo-ready date + that region's lead days; for Self, suggested reach = the cargo-ready date (you ship early to make the appointment). The suggestion is advice — the app reads the Actual reach date you commit.
SKUThe product code, picked from your master list so it always matches. The Product name fills in automatically next to it.
Always pick from the dropdowns — never type region names. The Destination cells only accept the exact names the app knows (like Delhi (DL)). Dates display Indian-style: 07-Jul-2026. Leave the Actual reach date and it stays red until filled — the app won't accept the file without it.

5. Job 2 — generating the inventory plan

Step 1. With the three Amazon CSVs uploaded and validated (as in Job 1), drop your completed workbook into Daily stock workbook (filled).
Step 2. Set Days of cover to plan for. Leave every other setting alone.
Step 3. Answer anything the app asks (§6 — most runs ask nothing), then click 🧮 Calculate plan.
Step 4. Review the on-screen results (§7) and click ⬇️ Download plan workbook. That's your finished inventory planning file.

6. Questions the app may ask, and every dropdown answer

The app never guesses on business judgment. When it needs yours, it pauses and asks on-screen. Here is every question and exactly what each answer does.

FC review — "MAA4 holds 36 sellable units but is not in the registration file"

A warehouse (FC) is holding your stock but isn't in Amazon's registration list — often a temporary GST/registration issue. You get two dropdowns. The first asks how to treat it this run:

ChoiceWhat it does / when to pick it
Map to a regionThe normal choice. Treat this FC as part of a region you pick, so its stock counts as supply there. A second dropdown appears to choose which region serves it (for MAA4: Chennai (TN)).
Treat as IXD cross-dockRare. Only if the FC genuinely just redistributes stock onward rather than serving customers. If unsure, this is not it.
IgnoreShow the stock but never count it in planning. Use when the stock is real but you don't want it influencing this plan.
AbortStop the run so you can fix the registration file instead. Pick this if you'd rather resolve it permanently now.

When you choose Map to a region, a second dropdown asks whether the stock is fulfillable:

FulfillableThe default. The stock can ship to customers, so count it as supply.
Not fulfillableThe stock is physically there but frozen (e.g. GST-blocked). It's shown but not counted in the plan.
Your answer applies to this run only. Once Amazon re-registers the FC and the registration file is refreshed, this question disappears for good.

Sales-window recency — "sales window ends …, stock is as of … — proceed?"

Your sales data is much older than your stock data (beyond the configured limit, normally 60 days). Sometimes that's deliberate — e.g. planning on last season's pattern. Tick the confirmation box to proceed; the choice is noted in the output, and the Sales-velocity tab shows the exact rates being applied. "Velocity" just means daily sales rate.

IXD stock at BLR4 — "how should this run treat it?"

Some of your stock is sitting at the BLR4 cross-dock — a hub Amazon uses to redistribute stock onward to regional warehouses. Where Amazon will send it is unpredictable: sometimes spread sensibly, sometimes the whole lot dumped into one region. Planning on that stock is therefore a bet, so the app pauses, shows you how many units and products are involved, and asks you to choose. As you pick each option, a plain explanation of exactly what it will do (with a live example from your own data) appears below the dropdown.

ChoiceWhat it does / when to pick it
IXD inbound — unpredictable. AbortThe default and the safe choice. Stops the run — no plan file. Come back in a few days: once Amazon dispatches the stock, enter that shipment on the workbook's In-transit tab (destination + reach date) and rerun; the plan then counts it in the right place at the right time.
Assume proper allocationBets that Amazon spreads each product across the regions that are short of it, in proportion to how short each one is. Your shipments to those regions shrink accordingly. Risk: if Amazon actually dumps it all in one place, the other regions can run out.
Dump into single neediest regionBets that Amazon sends each product's entire hub stock to the ONE region that needs it most — even past what it needs. That region gets little or nothing from you; every other region is planned as if it receives none.
Split across two neediest regionsBets that Amazon splits each product between its TWO neediest regions, in proportion to how short each is (uncapped — one may end up overstocked). If only one region is short, everything is assumed to go there.
Whatever you choose is recorded in the plan, and every affected line is flagged IXD_ASSUMED in Calculation_Details — so you can always see which quantities were a bet. When in doubt, Abort and replan in a few days.

ASIN judgment — a product holds stock under two SKUs

This happens during a listing suppression, when Amazon creates a temporary SKU for the same product. One product (one ASIN) then wears two SKU codes. Choose per product:

CombineThe suppression will lift soon — count both SKUs' stock together.
Only activeLong suppression — count only the live SKU's stock; the other SKU's stock is shown as excluded (not lost, just held aside this run).

7. The results screen & the downloaded plan

On screen after Calculate: four headline numbers (units planned, demand covered, demand excluded, stockout warnings); any yellow notices; Region priorities (which regions need attention first — Pri-1 = most urgent); the Sales velocity vs plan table; the full plan grid; and expanders for line-by-line detail, stockout warnings, and run information.

The downloaded Excel is ONE combined file — the inventory plan AND your daily stock report together. Tabs are colour-coded: AMBER = the plan (act on these), TEAL = daily stock report (keep these current), grey = README.

TabUse it for
README (grey)Which tab does what — start here.
Appointment plan (amber)THE deliverable — units to ship per product per region, with priorities. Act on this.
Calculation_Details (amber)Every number behind every quantity — daily sales, stock parts, rounding, flags (including any IXD_ASSUMED bets). The "why is it 32?" tab.
Sales velocity (amber)Per product per region: Plan qty | Velocity/day | Days cover, region blocks separated by lines, totals at the end.
Stock snapshot (teal)Read-only stock values. This is the tab to glance at on a phone — it shows in email/phone previews, where formula tabs can look blank until opened in Excel.
Reference, Current stock, In-transit, At the FC, Overall stock, EDD & Expiry (teal)Your daily working tabs, copied in from the workbook you uploaded — the same sheets, same formulas, so the file doubles as that day's stock report. They are a snapshot from generation time; regenerate for fresh data.
Opening on a phone? Formula-driven tabs (like Overall stock) can look empty in email/phone previews — the numbers appear once the file is opened in Excel or Sheets. The Stock snapshot tab always shows values, so use it for a mobile glance.
Days-cover higher than your setting is normal — full cartons buy extra days, and regions already holding stock show their true (large) coverage. But amber cells on the Sales-velocity tab mean possible OVERSTOCK — coverage more than 1.5× your setting. That's the app pointing at stock piling up against slow sales; review before shipping more there.

8. Common messages & fixes

MessageFix
"contains SKU(s) not in the master template"A new product isn't in the master list yet. An admin must add it (with its box size) to the master file — see the Admin guide. The app never guesses box sizes.
"the 'Stock as of' date (cell B1) is blank"Open the workbook's Current-stock tab and set the "Stock as of" date to your Amazon data's date, save, and re-upload.
"'Actual reach date' is …" (blank/unreadable)Every In-transit row needs a committed Actual reach date. Fill the red cells, save, re-upload.
"Destination '…' is not a known region"A workbook destination doesn't match a known region. Re-pick it from the dropdown. (Common city names are auto-corrected, but the dropdown is the safe way.)
"Sales window: only N days … below the 30-day minimum"Your sales file covers too short a period for a reliable average. Use a report covering at least 30 days.
"Validation failed — nothing was planned"Fix the red items listed above it and rerun. The app deliberately refuses to plan on faulty data.
Every stop names exactly what's wrong and what to do. Fix that one thing, rerun. The app is built to guide, not puzzle.
