1. What this app does

Every week you decide how much of each product to send to each of Amazon's regional warehouses. This app does that maths for you. You give it your recent sales, your current stock, and what's already on the way — it works out how fast each product sells in each region, how much stock you have coming, and how many boxes to ship so you don't run out (or overstock). It hands you back a ready-to-use plan in Excel.

It works in two stages: first you keep a stock workbook up to date (which doubles as your daily stock report), then you upload it to plan. The rest of this guide follows those two stages.

2. What you need before you start

Pull these three reports from Amazon Seller Central (all for the same date, ideally the same day you plan):

FileWhere in Seller Central
Sales export (CSV)Reports → Fulfilment → Amazon Fulfilled Shipments (or your monthly sales report)
General stock (CSV)Reports → Fulfilment → Manage FBA Inventory
FC-wise stock (CSV)Reports → Fulfilment → Inventory Ledger (Detailed view, Disposition = SELLABLE)
The three reference files — configurations, master template, FC registration — are already stored in the app. You don't upload those. They only change when your business changes (a new product, a new warehouse), and that's an admin task.

3. Stage 1 — Your daily stock workbook

This one Excel file is both your working stock record and the input the planner needs. You keep it current; the app reads it when you plan.

3.1 Generate a fresh workbook

Upload the three Amazon files first (the app needs them to build your workbook). Once they validate, open the "Need a fresh daily stock workbook?" section and click Generate & download. The app pre-fills your current stock from Amazon so you're not typing it from scratch.

[SCREENSHOT: the "Generate & download fresh workbook" button expanded in the app]

3.2 Fill it in

The workbook has these tabs you fill:

  • Current stock — pre-filled from Amazon; check the "Stock as of" date at the top is right.
  • In-transit — one row per shipment line: pick the destination, mode, and SKU from the dropdowns, then type quantity and cargo-ready date.
  • At the FC — shipments that have reached a warehouse and are being checked in; enter shipped and received quantities.
  • Reference — lead times per region; update if reality has changed.
Use the dropdowns — don't type region names. The Destination column has a dropdown offering the exact region names the app understands (like Delhi (DL)). If you type DELHI yourself, older versions rejected it. Always pick from the list — the cell will refuse a typed value that isn't on it.
[SCREENSHOT: the In-transit tab with the Destination dropdown open showing "Delhi (DL)", "Bombay (MH)"…]
Dates show in the clear Indian style — 07-Jul-2026 — so there's never any day/month confusion.

3.3 Save it — this IS your daily report too

The workbook's Overall stock tab rebuilds itself from what you fill, giving you a live picture of stock everywhere. Keep this file updated day to day; when it's time to plan, it's already ready. Commit the filled file back to your store (or keep it where you'll find it next week).

4. Stage 2 — Running the plan

Step 1. Upload the three Amazon files (sidebar). Wait for the green "✅ Amazon files validated".
Step 2. Upload your filled daily stock workbook.
Step 3. Set Days of cover — how many days of sales you want each region stocked for, on top of the shipping lead time. (30 is typical.)
Step 4. Answer any questions the app asks (see §6 below — most runs have none).
Step 5. Click 🧮 Calculate plan, review the on-screen summary, and click ⬇️ Download plan workbook.

5. Reading your plan (the output file)

The downloaded Excel has these tabs:

TabWhat it's for
READMEA short note on the file.
Appointment planThe main deliverable — how many units to ship to each region, per product, with priorities. This is what you act on.
Stock snapshotYour current / in-transit / at-FC stock, copied in so you can cross-check while working the plan.
Calculation DetailsEvery number behind the plan — daily sales, stock components, what was rounded, and any flags. Look here to understand "why this quantity".
Sales velocityPer region and per product: planned quantity, daily sales rate, and days of cover side by side.

Two things worth understanding

Days-cover can look higher than your setting. If you asked for 30 days but a row shows 90, that's usually because you already hold plenty of stock there, or a full carton buys more than 30 days. It's not an error — it's telling you that region is well covered.

Amber cells flag possible overstock. On the Sales-velocity tab, any days-cover well above your setting is highlighted amber. That's a nudge: a lot of stock sitting against slow sales. Worth a look before you send more.

6. Questions the app may ask you

Most runs go straight through. Occasionally the app pauses to ask — it never guesses on these:

QuestionWhy & what to do
An FC isn't recognised (e.g. MAA4 holds stock but isn't registered)A warehouse has stock but isn't in Amazon's registration (often a temporary GST situation). Choose Map to a region and pick which region serves it (e.g. Chennai), and whether its stock can currently ship. Once Amazon re-registers it and the registration file is refreshed, this stops appearing.
Your sales window looks oldIf your sales data is a lot older than your stock date, the app checks you meant to use it (maybe deliberately, as a seasonal baseline). Tick to confirm and it proceeds.
A product has two SKUs with stockHappens during a listing suppression (a temporary SKU appears). Choose whether to combine both SKUs' stock or use only the active one — depends on whether the suppression will lift soon.

7. Common messages & what to do

MessageWhat it means & the fix
"contains SKU(s) not in the master template"A product in your Amazon file isn't in the master list yet. It must be added to the master (an admin task) before it can be planned — the app won't guess a box size.
"Destination '…' is not a known region"A destination in your workbook doesn't match a known region. Use the dropdown to pick a valid one. (Newer versions auto-correct common city names, but the dropdown is the safe way.)
"effective dates are … days apart"Your stock files are from different dates. Re-pull them for the same date and re-upload.
"Validation failed — nothing was planned"One or more errors above must be fixed first. The app never plans on bad data — fix what's flagged and rerun.
Whenever something stops, the app tells you exactly what and why in plain terms. Read the red message, fix that one thing, and rerun — it's designed to guide you, not to puzzle you.
